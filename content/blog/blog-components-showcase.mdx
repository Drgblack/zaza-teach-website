This post demonstrates the enhanced blog components available for creating engaging, readable content that helps teachers learn more effectively.

## Rich Content Components

Our blog now supports several specialized components that make information more digestible and actionable.

<Tip>
These components break up large blocks of text and help readers focus on key information.
</Tip>

### Callout Boxes

Different types of callouts help highlight important information:

<Callout variant="info" title="Information">
Use info callouts for general tips and helpful context that supports the main content.
</Callout>

<Callout variant="success" title="Success Story">
Success callouts work great for highlighting wins, positive outcomes, or encouraging news.
</Callout>

<Callout variant="warning" title="Important Note">
Warning callouts draw attention to critical information or potential pitfalls to avoid.
</Callout>

## Reading Experience Features

This blog includes several enhancements for better reading:

- **Reading progress bar** at the top shows how far through the article you are
- **Table of contents** (visible on larger screens) helps navigate longer posts
- **Improved typography** with proper contrast ratios for accessibility
- **Responsive design** that works well on all devices

<Callout variant="success" title="Try It Out">
Scroll around this page to see the reading progress bar in action, and look for the table of contents on the right side if you're on a desktop.
</Callout>

The goal is to make educational content more engaging and easier to digest, helping teachers get the information they need quickly and effectively.
